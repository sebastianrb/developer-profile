About Me

Overview

Welcome! I am a New York-based front-end web developer who loves building complex, beautiful, and intuitive user interfaces. I was first exposed to the world of web development while working as a Junior Front-end Developer at Afinium, a tech startup. Since then, I have undertaken intensive study of front-end technologies as a student at Bov Academy, a development training program. I am passionate about travel and geography, and in the future I hope to work on web applications that utilize geolocation technologies and mapping.

I grew up in the suburbs of New York City and moved to Colorado when I was a teenager. After high school, I attended Dartmouth College and studied Economics and Arabic, and furthermore was fortunate enough to study both Arabic and Spanish abroad in Morocco and Mexico, respectively. Outside of web development, my interests and passions include photography (the header image is one of my photos), travel, cooking, languages, running, skiing, and weightlifting.

Bov Academy Student Site

I am a lead developer on the team that built the student profile site for Bov Academy. The site contains profiles for the school's student body and heavily utilizes multimedia and various animation effects. We built the site using vanilla JavaScript.

Vue.js Photo Gallery

This project is a photo gallery featuring some of my amateur photography work. The application is built using the Vue.js JavaScript framework.

Evolution UI Framework

Evolution UI is an open-source front-end framework comprising a library of innovative, unique web components, as well as a collection of standard components. I have contributed to and helped develop the project, and I now serve as a core maintainer.

Tic Tac Toe

This is a fully functional game of Tic Tac Toe played against the computer. The computer is moderately intelligent and will often try to block the player when the player is nearing victory. In addition, the computer will sometimes move first to keep things fair and interesting.

Jigsaw Puzzle

This is a working jigsaw puzzle. The user can ask for hints, reset the game as needed, and increase the difficulty. Be warned: the puzzle is fairly challenging, so pay close attention to the three hints!

Select Minor Projects

The portfolio includes a selection of minor projects. These projects are relatively small in scale and less complex than the others, but nonetheless exhibit important technologies and development techniques. They include a Handlebars photo album, an AJAX fact generator, and a Regex link harvester.
